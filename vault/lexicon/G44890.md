## Senses: 


### Sense  1.0: 

#### Definition: 

a person skilled in public speaking who spoke in a legal court either to defend a person or to prosecute someone 

#### Glosses: 

public speaker, orator, advocate, lawyer, spokesperson

#### Explanation: 

One of the most important aspects of Classical Greek learning was becoming skilled in the art of rhetoric, which enabled its practioners to argue their points clearly and persuasively before an audience.  Such "rhetors" were highly valued in public debates and, over time, their skills became increasingly used to represent clients in legal cases. The Jewish leaders employment of Tertullus to argue their case against Paul before Felix is an example of this legal advocacy by a rhetor
