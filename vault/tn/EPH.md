### εἰς αὐτὸ (1)
The connecting phrase **to this end** marks a goal or purpose relationship. The goal is what was just mentioned: to pray at all times in the Spirit. In order to do that, believers must be watchful and persevere in making requests for all the saints. Alternate Translation: “For this reason” or “In order to do that” (See: [[grammar-connect-logic-goal]])

## i5hm
### ἀγρυπνοῦντες ἐν πάσῃ προσκαρτερήσει καὶ δεήσει περὶ πάντων τῶν ἁγίων (1)
**Perseverance** is an abstract noun that can be translated with a verb. Alternate translation: “persevere in being alert, and pray for all God’s holy people” or “pray with constant alertness for all the believers” See the UST (See: [[figs-abstractnouns]])

# 6:20
## wx9k
### ὑπὲρ οὗ πρεσβεύω ἐν ἁλύσει (1)
The words **in chains** are a metonym for being in prison. Alternate translation: “because of which I am now in prison” (See: [[figs-metonymy]])

## pmm2
### ἵνα ἐν αὐτῷ παρρησιάσωμαι, ὡς δεῖ με λαλῆσαι (1)
The word **pray** is understood from verse 18. Alternate translation: “pray that whenever I teach the gospel, I will speak it as boldly as I should” or “pray that I may speak the gospel as boldly as I should” (See: [[figs-explicit]])

## abep
### ἵνα (1)
The connecting phrase **so that** introduces a goal relationship. The goal or purpose of the believers praying for Paul is to enable him to preach the gospel boldly even though he is in chains. (See: [[grammar-connect-logic-goal]])

## cdep
### ἐν αὐτῷ (1)
**It** refers back to the **message** in verse 19 that Paul wants to speak. If it is clearer in your language, you can repeat the word “message” here. Alternate translation: “in my message” (See: [[figs-pronouns]])

# 6:21
## aber

## abc2

Paul speaks of Tychicus as if he were a brother to all the other believers who were in God’s spiritual family. Alternate translation: “fellow believer” (See: [[figs-metaphor]])
